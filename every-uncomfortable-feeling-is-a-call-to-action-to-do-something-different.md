# Every Uncomfortable Feeling Is A Call To Action To Do Something Different

## Definition

Every uncomfortable feeling is a call to action to do something different.

Not some uncomfortable feelings.

Every uncomfortable feeling.

Frustration.

Disappointment.

Anger.

Fear.

Overwhelm.

Anxiety.

Stress.

Resentment.

They are all messages.

They are all feedback.

They are all trying to tell us something.

Most people try to get rid of uncomfortable feelings.

Few people stop long enough to ask:

"What is this feeling trying to tell me?"

---

## Feelings Are Feedback

Feelings are not punishments.

Feelings are not rewards.

Feelings are feedback.

They are part of our guidance system.

They are constantly communicating information to us.

Every feeling is attempting to move us.

Every feeling is attempting to teach us.

Every feeling is attempting to create awareness.

The question is not:

"How do I stop feeling this?"

The question is:

"What action is this feeling calling me to take?"

---

## The Call To Action

Every uncomfortable feeling contains a call to action.

Fear may be calling us to prepare.

Confusion may be calling us to gain clarity.

Frustration may be calling us to change our approach.

Disappointment may be calling us to adjust our expectations.

Resentment may be calling us to examine our rules.

Overwhelm may be calling us to simplify.

Stress may be calling us to realign.

The feeling is not the problem.

Ignoring the message is the problem.

---

## Most People Misinterpret The Signal

Imagine driving a car and a warning light comes on.

Most people would not smash the dashboard with a hammer.

Most people would not put tape over the light.

Most people would want to know what the warning light means.

Yet that is exactly what many people do with emotions.

They try to suppress them.

Distract themselves from them.

Ignore them.

Avoid them.

The feeling keeps returning because the message has not been received.

---

## Evolution Requires Feedback

In life either we evolve or else we dissolve.

Nothing stays the same.

Growth requires feedback.

Improvement requires feedback.

Transformation requires feedback.

Uncomfortable feelings are part of that feedback system.

Without them we would not know what needs attention.

Without them we would not know what needs changing.

Without them we would not know where growth is required.

---

## Ask Better Questions

Whenever an uncomfortable feeling appears, ask:

What is this feeling trying to teach me?

What is this feeling trying to show me?

What needs to change?

What action am I avoiding?

What would Love do here?

The quality of our questions determines the quality of our answers.

---

## Responsibility Creates Power

The moment we blame the feeling on someone else, we lose our power.

The moment we become curious about the feeling, we gain our power back.

Every uncomfortable feeling is giving us information.

Every uncomfortable feeling is offering guidance.

Every uncomfortable feeling is pointing toward growth.

If we listen.

---

## Action Resolves Emotion

Many uncomfortable feelings persist because no action has been taken.

The feeling arrives.

The message arrives.

But the action never occurs.

When appropriate action is taken, the feeling often changes.

Not because the feeling was wrong.

Because the message was received.

Action is often the completion of the emotional cycle.

---

## The Formula

Uncomfortable feeling.

Awareness.

Feedback.

Call to action.

New action.

New result.

New experience.

Growth.

Every uncomfortable feeling is a call to action to do something different.

The feeling is not happening to us.

The feeling is happening for us.

The question is:

What is it asking us to do?
