# Grand Dream Goals

## Foundational Doctrine of the Kurek Ashley Canon

---

# Definition

Throughout my life I have discovered that most people don't have a goal problem.

They have a dream problem.

Somewhere along the way they stopped dreaming.

They became practical.

Responsible.

Realistic.

They learned how to survive.

But they forgot how to dream.

I believe every human being needs something in their future that excites them.

Something that inspires them.

Something that pulls them forward.

Something that fills their heart with joy.

I call these Grand Dream Goals.

A Grand Dream Goal is not necessarily big.

It is not necessarily expensive.

It is not necessarily difficult.

In fact, some Grand Dream Goals cost nothing at all.

A Grand Dream Goal is defined by one thing:

> The amount of joy it brings you to experience.

Not someday.

Now.

When you think about it.

When you pursue it.

And when you achieve it.

Simply stated:

> A Grand Dream Goal is a goal that gives you the greatest amount of joy to experience.

---

# Most People Have Been Taught The Wrong Way To Set Goals

Many people choose goals based upon what they think they should want.

More money.

More status.

More approval.

More recognition.

More possessions.

There is nothing wrong with any of those things.

The problem is that many people never stop to ask themselves:

> Is this actually my dream?

Or have I simply adopted someone else's definition of success?

One of the saddest things in life is watching someone spend years climbing a ladder only to discover it was leaning against the wrong wall.

A Grand Dream Goal must be your dream.

Not your parents' dream.

Not society's dream.

Not your friends' dream.

Not your partner's dream.

Your dream.

Because only your dream has the power to fully awaken your spirit.

---

# Joy Is The Compass

One of the greatest gifts we have been given is joy.

Joy tells us when we are moving toward something that is aligned with who we truly are.

It is guidance.

It is feedback.

It is wisdom.

Many people ignore joy because they have been taught that success should be difficult.

I disagree.

Growth can be challenging.

Life can be challenging.

But your dream should bring you alive.

When you think about your Grand Dream Goal, you should feel something.

Excitement.

Anticipation.

Inspiration.

Possibility.

Joy.

If it doesn't light you up, it may not be your dream.

---

# The Three Stages Of Joy

A true Grand Dream Goal creates joy in three places.

First, it brings joy when you think about it.

The very idea excites you.

Second, it brings joy while you are pursuing it.

You enjoy becoming the person required to achieve it.

Third, it brings joy when you achieve it.

Many goals only satisfy the third stage.

Grand Dream Goals satisfy all three.

This distinction is important.

Because many people spend years pursuing goals they hate in order to experience a few moments of happiness at the end.

That is not a Grand Dream Goal.

That is a transaction.

A Grand Dream Goal is an experience.

---

# The Journey Becomes The Destination

One of the most important lessons I have learned is this:

> The way you pursue your dream determines the experience of achieving it.

Many people believe:

"I'll be happy when I get there."

So they pursue their goals through stress.

Pressure.

Sacrifice.

Frustration.

Overwhelm.

And then they are surprised when achievement feels empty.

The journey became the habit.

The destination became another version of the journey.

If you want your dream to bring you joy when you achieve it, you must pursue it in a joyful way.

Joy is not meant to be postponed.

Joy is meant to accompany you along the path.

---

# Grand Dream Goals Expand Life

Grand Dream Goals make life larger.

They expand possibility.

They expand awareness.

They expand enthusiasm.

They expand hope.

When people have something meaningful to look forward to, life feels different.

Energy increases.

Creativity increases.

Resilience increases.

The dream begins pulling them forward.

Not through force.

Through inspiration.

---

# The Real Purpose Of A Grand Dream Goal

Most people think the purpose of a goal is achievement.

I believe the purpose of a Grand Dream Goal is transformation.

Not transformation through suffering.

Transformation through expansion.

The dream invites you to grow.

To learn.

To become more.

To develop gifts you may never have discovered otherwise.

The dream changes you long before it is achieved.

In many cases, the person you become is far more valuable than the outcome itself.

---

# Grand Dream Goals And Love

Love expands life.

Fear contracts life.

Grand Dream Goals are born from expansion.

They come from possibility.

Creativity.

Contribution.

Expression.

Love.

The dream is not asking you to prove your worth.

You were already worthy.

The dream is inviting you to express more of who you are.

That is very different.

---

# Grand Dream Goals And Purpose

Purpose is the rudder.

Grand Dream Goals are the destinations we choose to explore.

Purpose gives direction.

Grand Dream Goals create enthusiasm.

Together they create momentum.

Purpose tells us why.

Grand Dream Goals give us something exciting to move toward.

Together they make life an adventure.

---

# Practical Application

Ask yourself:

1. What would bring me the greatest amount of joy to experience?
2. What dream keeps returning to me?
3. What excites me when I think about it?
4. What dream feels authentically mine?
5. What would I pursue even if nobody applauded?
6. How can I pursue this dream in a joyful way?
7. What action could I take today?
8. How would love respond?

Then begin.

Not because the dream guarantees happiness.

But because the pursuit itself becomes part of the happiness.

# The Transformer Principle

Life was never meant to be endured.

Life was meant to be experienced.

Too many people have stopped dreaming.

They have settled for what is practical rather than what is possible.

A Grand Dream Goal reconnects us with aliveness.

With enthusiasm.

With purpose.

With joy.

The measure of a Grand Dream Goal is not how big it is.

The measure is how much joy it brings to your life.

Because ultimately:

> A Grand Dream Goal is not the goal that impresses others.

> A Grand Dream Goal is the goal that brings you the greatest amount of joy to experience.

And when you pursue it joyfully, it has the power to transform not only your future, but your present as well.
