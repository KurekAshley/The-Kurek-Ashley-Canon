# Stories Create Drama Glue

## Definition

Stories create drama glue.

They keep you stuck in the story.

And they keep everyone else stuck in the story too.

Most people do not get stuck because of what happened.

They get stuck because of the story they keep telling about what happened.

---

## The Story Is Not Real

The story is not even real.

It is an edited interpretation of what happened.

You take an event.

You remove parts of it.

You add meaning to it.

You create conclusions about it.

Then you tell the story over and over again until you believe it is the truth.

But it is not the truth.

It is your interpretation.

It is your perception.

---

## Most Stories Are Disempowering

The majority of people tell disempowering stories.

Stories about who hurt them.

Stories about who betrayed them.

Stories about what went wrong.

Stories about why life has been unfair.

Stories about why they cannot have what they want.

And every time they tell those stories, they give their power away.

The story becomes stronger.

The identity becomes stronger.

The limitation becomes stronger.

The drama glue gets thicker.

---

## Drama Glue

The reason I call it drama glue is because once people start telling drama stories, they get stuck in the drama.

Not only does the person telling the story get stuck.

The person listening gets stuck too.

The drama glue spreads.

It pulls everyone into the story.

It keeps people focused on the problem instead of the solution.

It keeps people focused on the past instead of the future.

It keeps people focused on the pain instead of the possibility.

---

## The Past Equals Pain

When you keep replaying painful stories from the past, your mind relives the experience.

Your body reacts as if it is happening again.

The emotions come back.

The reactions come back.

The behaviors come back.

And then the results come back.

People think they are remembering the past.

What they are really doing is recreating it.

---

## Looking Out The Back Window

Living through your stories is like driving a car while looking out the back window.

You cannot see where you are going.

You cannot see the opportunities in front of you.

You cannot see the possibilities in front of you.

You keep crashing into the future because you are staring at the past.

---

## Stop Telling Drama Stories

Stop telling stories that disempower you.

Stop telling stories about why it cannot happen.

Stop telling stories about why you are different.

Stop telling stories about why life has been unfair.

Stop telling stories about what somebody did to you twenty years ago.

Those stories do not serve you.

Those stories steal your power.

Those stories create drama glue.

---

## Tell Empowering Stories

Instead, tell stories that empower you.

Tell stories about what you learned.

Tell stories about what you overcame.

Tell stories about how you found a way.

Tell stories about your victories.

Tell stories about your courage.

Tell stories about your growth.

Tell stories that move you forward.

Not stories that keep you stuck.

---

## It Is Never Too Late To Have A Great Childhood

Most people spend their lives telling stories about why their childhood damaged them.

I teach something different.

It is never too late to have a great childhood.

Start telling different stories.

Start focusing on what was good.

Start focusing on what made you stronger.

Start focusing on what helped shape you into who you are today.

Because whatever stories you repeat, you reinforce.

---

## The Choice

Every story either empowers you or disempowers you.

Every story either moves you forward or keeps you stuck.

Every story either creates possibility or creates limitation.

The question is not:

"What story happened?"

The question is:

"What story am I choosing to tell?"

Because stories create identity.

Identity creates behavior.

Behavior creates results.

Choose stories that empower you.

Let go of stories that create drama glue.
