# All Conflicts In Life Are Rules Conflicts

## Definition

All conflicts in life are rules conflicts.

Every conflict.

Internal conflict.

Relationship conflict.

Business conflict.

Family conflict.

Team conflict.

Emotional conflict.

All conflict occurs because rules are in conflict.

Most people think they have a people problem.

Most people think they have a situation problem.

Most people think they have a circumstance problem.

What they actually have is a rules problem.

---

## What Are Rules?

Rules are the conditions we create for how life should be.

Rules determine what things mean.

Rules determine what is right.

Rules determine what is wrong.

Rules determine what makes us happy.

Rules determine what makes us unhappy.

Rules determine what we accept.

Rules determine what we reject.

Whether we realize it or not, every one of us is living by a set of rules.

---

## Internal Conflict

The greatest conflicts are often not with other people.

They are within ourselves.

Part of us wants one thing.

Part of us wants something else.

One rule says:

"Play it safe."

Another rule says:

"Go for it."

One rule says:

"Don't risk failure."

Another rule says:

"Follow your purpose."

The conflict is not between two realities.

The conflict is between two rules.

---

## Relationship Conflict

Most relationship conflicts are rules conflicts.

One person has one set of rules.

The other person has another.

One person believes respect means one thing.

The other person believes respect means something else.

One person has rules about money.

The other person has different rules about money.

One person has rules about communication.

The other person has different rules about communication.

Neither realizes they are fighting over rules.

They think they are fighting over the issue.

The issue is rarely the issue.

The rules are the issue.

---

## Emotional Conflict

Emotions are feedback.

They are not punishment.

They are not rewards.

They are feedback.

When reality matches our rules, we tend to feel good.

When reality violates our rules, we tend to feel bad.

The emotion is revealing the conflict.

The emotion is showing us where the rule exists.

The emotion is the signal.

Not the problem.

---

## Empowering Rules And Disempowering Rules

Some rules support our growth.

Some rules limit our growth.

Some rules create freedom.

Some rules create suffering.

For example:

"I must be perfect before I can succeed."

That rule creates conflict.

"I must never fail."

That rule creates conflict.

"Everyone must approve of me."

That rule creates conflict.

The tighter the rule, the greater the conflict.

The healthier the rule, the greater the freedom.

---

## Values And Rules

Our highest values create our strongest rules.

Whatever is important to us will have rules attached to it.

The more important something is, the stronger the rules tend to become.

This is why values work is so important.

When we understand our values, we begin to understand our rules.

When we understand our rules, we begin to understand our conflicts.

---

## Resolving Conflict

Most people try to solve conflict at the surface.

They argue about circumstances.

They argue about events.

They argue about behaviors.

The real question is:

What rule is creating this conflict?

Once the rule is identified, the conflict often begins to dissolve.

Because the rule can now be examined.

The rule can be challenged.

The rule can be replaced.

The rule can be upgraded.

---

## Personal Transformation

One of the fastest ways to change our life is to change our rules.

Change the rule and the experience changes.

Change the rule and the emotion changes.

Change the rule and the behavior changes.

Change the rule and the result changes.

The conflict was never in life.

The conflict was in the rule.

---

## The Formula

Rules create meaning.

Meaning creates emotion.

Emotion influences behavior.

Behavior creates results.

Whenever conflict appears, look for the rule.

Because all conflicts in life are rules conflicts.
