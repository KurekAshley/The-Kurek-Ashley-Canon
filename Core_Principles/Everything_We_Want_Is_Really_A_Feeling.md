# Everything We Want Is Really A Feeling

## Foundational Doctrine of the Kurek Ashley Canon

---

# Definition

Most people spend their lives pursuing things.

Money.

Success.

Recognition.

Relationships.

Achievement.

Possessions.

Status.

Security.

Yet what most people fail to realize is that they are not actually pursuing those things.

They are pursuing the feelings they believe those things will give them.

The thing itself is rarely the goal.

The feeling associated with the thing is the goal.

Simply stated:

> Everything we want is really a feeling.

This understanding changes everything.

Because once we understand what we are truly seeking, we stop chasing shadows and begin creating a life that genuinely fulfills us.

---

# The Great Misunderstanding

People often say:

"I want more money."

Do they?

Not usually.

What they want is what they believe money will provide.

Freedom.

Security.

Peace of mind.

Choices.

Contribution.

Likewise, people say:

"I want a bigger business."

"I want a better relationship."

"I want more success."

"I want recognition."

Yet beneath every desire is a feeling.

Every goal contains an emotional destination.

The problem is that many people become so focused on the vehicle that they forget the destination.

---

# The Feeling Beneath The Goal

Whenever someone tells me what they want, I become curious about something deeper.

I want to know:

> What will that give you?

And then:

> What will that give you?

And then:

> What will that give you?

Eventually we arrive at the real answer.

A feeling.

Love.

Connection.

Freedom.

Fulfillment.

Peace.

Joy.

Significance.

Purpose.

Contribution.

The feeling is almost always the true objective.

The external goal is simply the route people believe will get them there.

---

# Why Achievement Sometimes Feels Empty

One of the most confusing experiences people have is achieving a goal they desperately wanted and then discovering they are not happy.

They finally earn the money.

Build the business.

Win the award.

Buy the house.

Reach the milestone.

Yet the fulfillment they expected never arrives.

Why?

Because they were pursuing the object instead of the feeling.

They assumed the feeling would automatically appear once the goal was achieved.

Sometimes it does.

Sometimes it doesn't.

The feeling was never inside the achievement.

The feeling was inside them.

---

# The Success Trap

Many people spend years trapped in what I call "when-then living."

When I make more money, then I'll be happy.

When I find the perfect relationship, then I'll feel loved.

When I lose the weight, then I'll feel confident.

When I become successful, then I'll feel worthy.

The challenge is that happiness continually moves into the future.

Life becomes a postponement of fulfillment.

We begin living for someday instead of today.

We make our emotional wellbeing conditional.

And conditions rarely stay satisfied for long.

---

# The Pursuit Of Feelings

Every human behavior is driven by a desire to experience a feeling or avoid a feeling.

Think about it.

People pursue:

- Success to feel accomplished.
- Wealth to feel free.
- Relationships to feel connected.
- Contribution to feel meaningful.
- Adventure to feel alive.
- Recognition to feel valued.
- Growth to feel expanded.
- Love to feel complete.

The feeling is always driving the behavior.

Whether people realize it or not.

---

# Feelings Create Direction

Once we understand the feelings we are truly seeking, our choices become more intentional.

We stop asking:

> What do I want?

And begin asking:

> How do I want to feel?

That question often reveals deeper truths.

It helps separate authentic desires from borrowed desires.

Many people spend years chasing goals that belong to someone else.

Society's goals.

Family's goals.

Culture's goals.

Comparison's goals.

Yet their soul longs for something entirely different.

The desired feeling often points toward what truly matters.

---

# Happiness Is Not The Reward

One of the biggest misconceptions in personal development is the belief that happiness is the reward for success.

I have found the opposite to be true.

Happiness is often the fuel for success.

People who learn how to experience joy, gratitude, love, and fulfillment now tend to create better outcomes later.

Why?

Because the frequency they live in influences the reality they create.

As we teach throughout this Canon:

> Thought creates frequency.

> Frequency creates form.

The feelings we cultivate shape the experiences we attract and create.

---

# The Connection To Frequency

Feelings are not merely emotional experiences.

They are energetic experiences.

They influence perception.

Behavior.

Choices.

Relationships.

Expectations.

Action.

When people consistently experience fear, scarcity, resentment, or hopelessness, those states influence the reality they create.

When people consistently experience gratitude, love, possibility, purpose, and joy, those states influence reality differently.

The feeling is not separate from creation.

The feeling is part of creation.

---

# The Highest Feelings

Not all feelings produce the same quality of life.

Some contract us.

Some expand us.

Fear contracts.

Love expands.

Resentment contracts.

Gratitude expands.

Judgment contracts.

Compassion expands.

Scarcity contracts.

Abundance expands.

The feelings that create the greatest fulfillment are often the feelings that expand our connection with ourselves and others.

---

# Love Is The Ultimate Feeling

If we continue tracing human desires deeper and deeper, we often arrive at one destination.

Love.

The desire to love.

The desire to be loved.

The desire to belong.

The desire to connect.

The desire to contribute.

The desire to matter.

Love sits beneath many of the goals people pursue.

This is one reason the question:

> How Would Love Respond?

is so powerful.

It immediately shifts attention toward one of the highest frequencies available to us.

---

# The Birthright Of Happiness

For much of my life I have taught something that many people find difficult to accept.

Happiness is your birthright.

Not because life is always easy.

Not because pain doesn't exist.

Not because challenges disappear.

But because happiness is not something we earn.

It is something we cultivate.

It is a way of being.

Many people spend years trying to earn what is already available.

They are seeking externally what must ultimately be generated internally.

---

# Purpose, Feelings, And Fulfillment

Purpose and feelings are deeply connected.

Purpose creates direction.

Feelings create experience.

Together they create fulfillment.

A life without purpose often feels empty.

A life without joy often feels exhausting.

A life with both becomes meaningful.

The goal is not merely achievement.

The goal is a meaningful life filled with love, growth, contribution, and joy.

---

# Practical Application

Ask yourself:

1. What do I want most right now?
2. What feeling do I believe that will give me?
3. Can I begin cultivating that feeling today?
4. What actions align with that feeling?
5. What choices would support that feeling?
6. What would love encourage me to do?
7. Am I pursuing someone else's dream or my own?

Then begin living from the feeling instead of chasing it.

---

# The Transformer Principle

Most people spend their lives chasing things.

Very few spend their lives understanding why they are chasing them.

The moment we understand that everything we want is really a feeling, our relationship with life changes.

We stop postponing happiness.

We stop making fulfillment conditional.

We stop waiting for the future to give us permission to feel alive.

Instead, we begin cultivating the very qualities we seek.

And from that place, we create a life that is not only successful, but deeply meaningful.

Because in the end:

> The things we pursue are rarely what we truly want.

> What we truly want is how we believe those things will make us feel.
