# In Order For Things To Change, I Must Change

## Definition

In order for things to change, I must change.

Not them.

Not the economy.

Not the government.

Not the market.

Not my spouse.

Not my parents.

Not my children.

Not my circumstances.

Me.

This is one of the most empowering realizations a person can have.

Because the moment I realize I am the variable, I regain my power.

---

## The Common Mistake

Most people spend their lives trying to change things outside themselves.

They try to change other people.

They try to change circumstances.

They try to change conditions.

They try to change the environment.

And while there are times when those things may change, lasting transformation always begins somewhere else.

It begins with us.

Because wherever we go, we take ourselves with us.

---

## Results Are Feedback

Every result in life is feedback.

Results are not rewards.

Results are not punishments.

Results are feedback.

Results reveal our current level of thinking.

Results reveal our current level of belief.

Results reveal our current self-image.

Results reveal our current frequency.

If we want different results, we must create a different frequency.

If we want a different frequency, we must change the source.

We must change ourselves.

---

## The Person Creates The Result

People often want a new result without becoming a new person.

That rarely works.

The result is the effect.

The person is the cause.

Most people are focused on the effect.

Successful people focus on the cause.

If I become the person capable of creating the result, the result becomes a natural by-product.

---

## Frequency Must Change

Everything is frequency first.

Whatever frequency we are on determines what we can receive.

If I continue thinking the same thoughts, acting the same way, making the same decisions, and holding the same beliefs, I remain on the same frequency.

And if I remain on the same frequency, I continue receiving the same results.

To receive something different, I must become different.

I must change my frequency.

---

## Self-Image Sets The Ceiling

We cannot outperform our self-image.

Our self-image determines what feels normal.

Our self-image determines what we allow.

Our self-image determines what we expect.

If I want my life to expand, my self-image must expand.

If I want greater results, I must become a greater version of myself.

The external result follows the internal transformation.

---

## Identity Before Outcome

Most people ask:

How do I get the result?

A better question is:

Who must I become?

Every meaningful goal requires an identity shift.

Every meaningful dream requires growth.

Every meaningful result requires transformation.

The person who achieves the goal is not the same person who first set the goal.

The journey changes them.

That is one of the reasons goals are so valuable.

They give us a target for growth.

---

## Stop Waiting

Many people are waiting for life to change before they change.

That is backwards.

They say:

When I make more money, I'll feel confident.

When the relationship improves, I'll be happier.

When the opportunity comes, I'll step up.

Life doesn't work that way.

We change first.

Then the results begin to change.

---

## The Mirror

Life is like a mirror.

The mirror does not change first.

We do.

If you smile at the mirror, the reflection smiles back.

If you change, the reflection changes.

The same principle applies to life.

The outside world often reflects what is happening inside us.

---

## Personal Responsibility

Responsibility is not blame.

Responsibility means response-ability.

The ability to choose our response.

The ability to choose our thoughts.

The ability to choose our actions.

The ability to choose our focus.

The ability to choose who we become.

The more responsibility we take, the more power we gain.

---

## The Formula

New thoughts.

New beliefs.

New expectations.

New self-image.

New behavior.

New frequency.

New results.

Most people try to start with the results.

Transformation starts with the person.

In order for things to change, I must change.

And the moment I change, everything becomes possible.
