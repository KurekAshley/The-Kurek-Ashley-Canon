# Meaning Creates Experience

## Foundational Doctrine of the Kurek Ashley Canon

---

# Definition

Life does not determine our experience.

Meaning determines our experience.

Events happen.

Circumstances occur.

Challenges arise.

Opportunities appear.

But the event itself is rarely what creates suffering, joy, empowerment, fear, gratitude, resilience, or growth.

What creates the experience is the meaning we attach to the event.

Simply stated:

> Meaning creates experience.

Change the meaning and the experience changes.

Change the experience and life begins to change.

---

# The Great Human Gift

Human beings possess a remarkable ability.

We can assign meaning.

This ability is one of our greatest gifts.

It is also one of our greatest responsibilities.

At any moment we can choose what something means.

That choice shapes our emotional experience.

That emotional experience influences our actions.

Those actions influence our results.

Most people spend their lives believing events create emotions.

Transformational living begins when we recognize that meaning creates emotions.

---

# The Day My Life Changed

Throughout life there are moments that divide existence into "before" and "after."

One of those moments for me occurred during the filming of *Delta Force 2*.

A helicopter crashed.

A dear friend died.

Lives changed forever.

People often assume that events alone determine the future.

If that were true, every person involved would have experienced identical outcomes.

They did not.

The event was the same.

The meanings were different.

Those meanings created different emotional experiences, different choices, and ultimately different futures.

The event mattered.

The meaning mattered more.

That lesson became one of the foundations of my life's work.

---

# Events Are Neutral Until Meaning Is Applied

An event has no emotional meaning until meaning is assigned.

Consider rain.

To a farmer it may represent abundance.

To a bride planning an outdoor wedding it may represent disappointment.

To a child it may represent play.

The event is identical.

The experience is different.

Why?

Meaning.

This principle applies to virtually every area of life.

Success.

Failure.

Rejection.

Achievement.

Loss.

Challenge.

Opportunity.

Meaning determines experience.

---

# The Story We Tell Ourselves

Every human being is continuously telling stories.

These stories explain:

- Who we are.
- What life means.
- What is possible.
- What is fair.
- What is deserved.
- What the future holds.

Most people never question their stories.

They simply live inside them.

Yet stories are nothing more than meanings repeated often enough to feel true.

When the story changes, life changes.

---

# Meaning Creates Emotion

Emotions are often misunderstood.

People frequently believe emotions are created by circumstances.

More accurately, emotions are created by meanings.

Consider the difference between these meanings:

### Meaning One

"This challenge proves I am not capable."

### Meaning Two

"This challenge is preparing me for something greater."

The circumstance is identical.

The emotion is completely different.

The resulting behavior is completely different.

The future that emerges is completely different.

---

# The Prison And The Freedom

Meaning can imprison.

Meaning can liberate.

A single disempowering meaning can limit a person for decades.

A single empowering meaning can transform an entire life.

Many people spend years carrying meanings such as:

- I am not enough.
- I am unlucky.
- People cannot be trusted.
- Success is difficult.
- Love always ends in pain.

Those meanings eventually become identities.

Those identities become realities.

Transformation begins by challenging the meaning.

---

# Every Experience Contains A Gift

One of the distinctions I have taught for decades is this:

Every experience contains a gift.

The gift may not be immediately visible.

The lesson may not be immediately obvious.

The growth may not be immediately apparent.

Yet life is constantly inviting us to discover meaning that empowers rather than meaning that diminishes.

The question is not:

> Why did this happen to me?

The more powerful question is:

> What can this experience teach me?

That question changes meaning.

Meaning changes experience.

---

# The Love Question

At the heart of my work is a simple question:

> How Would Love Respond?

This question is transformational because it immediately changes meaning.

Conflict takes on a different meaning.

Failure takes on a different meaning.

Fear takes on a different meaning.

Pain takes on a different meaning.

People take on a different meaning.

Life takes on a different meaning.

And when meaning changes, experience changes.

# Meaning And Self-Image

Repeated meanings eventually become beliefs.

Repeated beliefs become identity.

Identity becomes self-image.

```text
Meaning
    ↓
Belief
    ↓
Identity
    ↓
Self-Image
```

This is why meaning is so important.

The meanings we repeatedly assign eventually become the people we believe ourselves to be.

---

# Meaning And Transformation

Transformation is often less about changing circumstances and more about changing interpretation.

Two individuals can stand in the same place.

One sees devastation.

One sees opportunity.

One sees failure.

One sees feedback.

One sees punishment.

One sees preparation.

The event remains the same.

Meaning changes.

Life changes.

---

# Practical Application

Whenever facing challenge, disappointment, fear, frustration, loss, or uncertainty, ask:

1. What meaning am I giving this?
2. Is that meaning empowering or disempowering?
3. Is it absolutely true?
4. What other meaning could be equally true?
5. Which meaning serves my growth?
6. Which meaning would love choose?

Then act from that meaning.

Transformation begins in that moment.
