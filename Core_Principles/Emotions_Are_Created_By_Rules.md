# Emotions Are Created By Rules

## Foundational Doctrine of the Kurek Ashley Canon

---

# Definition

Most people believe emotions are created by circumstances.

They are not.

Emotions are largely created by the meanings and rules we attach to circumstances.

Rules are the conscious and unconscious conditions we establish for how life, people, and ourselves should behave.

When reality aligns with our rules, we experience one emotional response.

When reality violates our rules, we experience another.

Simply stated:

> Emotions are created by rules.

Change the rules.

Change the emotional experience.

---

# The Great Misunderstanding

People often say:

- "You made me angry."
- "She hurt my feelings."
- "That situation made me frustrated."
- "My boss stresses me out."

These statements assume emotions are created externally.

Yet two people can experience the exact same circumstance and feel completely different emotions.

Why?

Because they are operating from different rules.

The event is not creating the emotion.

The rule is.

---

# What Is A Rule?

A rule is a condition.

A requirement.

A standard.

A belief about how something must occur before we can feel a certain way.

Examples:

- People must respect me.
- My children should always listen.
- Business should be easy.
- My partner must understand me.
- I should never make mistakes.
- Life should be fair.

The more rigid the rule, the more emotional suffering it tends to create.

---

# The Emotional Formula

Most people believe:

```text
Event
    ↓
Emotion
```

The actual process is usually:

```text
Event
    ↓
Meaning
    ↓
Rule
    ↓
Emotion
```

This distinction changes everything.

Because if rules create emotions, then emotions can be transformed.

---

# A Simple Example

Imagine someone criticizes you.

One person has the rule:

> Everyone must approve of me.

The result may be:

- Hurt
- Rejection
- Anger
- Defensiveness

Another person has the rule:

> Not everyone needs to agree with me.

The result may be:

- Curiosity
- Reflection
- Neutrality
- Growth

The event is identical.

The rule is different.

The emotion changes.

---

# Hidden Rules Create Hidden Suffering

Many rules operate below conscious awareness.

People rarely wake up and say:

> I have a rule that everyone must appreciate me.

Yet they live as though that rule exists.

When appreciation disappears, suffering appears.

The same pattern occurs in nearly every area of life.

Relationships.

Business.

Health.

Parenting.

Leadership.

Personal development.

Unconscious rules create unconscious suffering.

Awareness creates freedom.

---

# The Perfection Trap

One of the most destructive rules is perfection.

Examples include:

- I must never fail.
- I must always get it right.
- I should already know this.
- I must perform perfectly.

Perfection-based rules create:

- Anxiety
- Fear
- Procrastination
- Self-criticism
- Shame

The pursuit of excellence creates growth.

The demand for perfection often creates suffering.

---

# The Happiness Trap

Many people unknowingly create impossible rules for happiness.

Examples:

- I'll be happy when I earn more money.
- I'll be happy when I find the perfect relationship.
- I'll be happy when my business succeeds.
- I'll be happy when people appreciate me.

The problem is not the goal.

The problem is the rule.

Happiness becomes conditional.

Life becomes a constant pursuit rather than an experience.

One of the central teachings of this Canon is:

> Happiness is your birthright.

Not your reward.

Not your destination.

Your birthright.

---

# Rules And Relationships

Relationships often expose hidden rules faster than any other area of life.

Examples:

- My partner must always understand me.
- My children should never disappoint me.
- Friends must always agree with me.
- People should know what I need without me telling them.

These rules create expectations.

Expectations create emotional reactions.

Emotional reactions influence behavior.

Behavior influences relationships.

Many relationship struggles are not caused by people.

They are caused by rules.

---

# Rules And Identity

Rules often become part of self-image.

Consider these rules:

- I must always be strong.
- I must never show weakness.
- I must always succeed.
- I must always have the answers.

Eventually these rules become identity.

Identity becomes pressure.

Pressure becomes emotional suffering.

The individual becomes trapped inside a version of themselves that no longer serves them.

---

# Rules And Leadership

Leaders operate from rules whether they realize it or not.

A leader who believes:

> People should never make mistakes.

creates a very different culture than a leader who believes:

> Mistakes are opportunities for learning.

Rules shape culture.

Culture shapes behavior.

Behavior shapes results.

Leadership is often the management of rules.

---

# The Freedom Of Better Rules

The objective is not to eliminate rules.

The objective is to create empowering rules.

Disempowering Rule:

> I must succeed every time.

Empowering Rule:

> I am committed to learning every time.

Disempowering Rule:

> Everyone must like me.

Empowering Rule:

> I will remain true to my values.

Disempowering Rule:

> Life must be easy.

Empowering Rule:

> Growth often comes through challenge.

The quality of our rules influences the quality of our lives.

---

# The Love Question

One of the fastest ways to transform a rule is to ask:

> How Would Love Respond?

Love often operates from different rules.

Fear says:

> I must be right.

Love says:

> I seek understanding.

Fear says:

> People must never disappoint me.

Love says:

> People are human.

Fear says:

> I am not enough.

Love says:

> I am worthy exactly as I am while continuing to grow.

Love changes the rules.

The rules change the emotions.

The emotions change the experience.

---

# Practical Application

When experiencing a strong emotional reaction, ask:

1. What am I feeling?
2. What event triggered it?
3. What meaning did I assign to the event?
4. What rule is being violated?
5. Is that rule serving me?
6. What would be a more empowering rule?
7. How would love view this situation?

Then consciously choose a new rule.

The emotional experience will begin changing immediately.

# Emotional Mastery

Emotional mastery is not the suppression of emotion.

It is not pretending.

It is not denial.

Emotional mastery is understanding how emotions are created and consciously participating in that process.

The goal is not to feel less.

The goal is to become free.

Free to respond rather than react.

Free to choose rather than be controlled.

Free to create rather than repeat.

---

# The Transformer Principle

Most people spend their lives trying to manage emotions.

Transformational living begins by managing the rules that create those emotions.

When rules change:

Emotions change.

Behavior changes.

Relationships change.

Results change.

Life changes.

That is why:

> Emotions are created by rules.
