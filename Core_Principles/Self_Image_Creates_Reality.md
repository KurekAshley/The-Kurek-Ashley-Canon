# Self-Image Creates Reality

## Foundational Doctrine of the Kurek Ashley Canon

---

# Definition

People do not consistently perform at the level of their potential.

They perform at the level of their self-image.

The self-image acts as an internal blueprint that governs behavior, expectations, decisions, relationships, achievement, and ultimately the reality a person experiences.

Simply stated:

> Self-image creates reality.

A person's life tends to move in the direction of who they believe themselves to be.

---

# The Hidden Force Behind Results

Most people focus on results.

They focus on:

- Income
- Relationships
- Health
- Business
- Performance
- Happiness

Yet results are rarely the true issue.

Results are symptoms.

The deeper issue is identity.

Behind every result exists a self-image attempting to remain consistent with itself.

This is why people often sabotage opportunities, relationships, finances, and success.

The problem is rarely capability.

The problem is identity.

---

# The Thermostat Principle

A thermostat regulates temperature.

Self-image regulates reality.

When life rises above an individual's self-image, behavior often pulls them back down.

When life falls below an individual's self-image, behavior often elevates them again.

The self-image continuously seeks consistency.

This explains why some people repeatedly:

- Create the same problems.
- Enter the same relationships.
- Experience the same frustrations.
- Earn similar income levels.
- Repeat similar patterns.

The pattern is not random.

The self-image is maintaining equilibrium.

---

# The Story Of Who We Believe We Are

Every human being carries an internal story.

That story answers questions such as:

- Who am I?
- What am I capable of?
- What do I deserve?
- What is possible for me?
- How valuable am I?
- How lovable am I?
- How successful can I become?

Most people never consciously create these answers.

They inherit them.

The self-image is often built from:

- Childhood experiences
- Family beliefs
- Culture
- Education
- Successes
- Failures
- Emotional experiences
- Meanings assigned to events

Over time these experiences form identity.

Identity becomes self-image.

Self-image becomes reality.

---

# Why Knowledge Is Not Enough

One of the greatest frustrations in personal development is that people often know what to do but fail to do it.

Knowledge is rarely the issue.

Identity is the issue.

A person may know how to become healthier.

A person may know how to improve relationships.

A person may know how to grow a business.

Yet if the self-image does not support those actions, lasting change becomes difficult.

People act consistently with who they believe they are.

Not with what they know.

---

# The Evidence Loop

Self-image creates a powerful cycle.

```text
Self-Image
      ↓
Behavior
      ↓
Results
      ↓
Evidence
      ↓
Self-Image
```

The results become evidence.

The evidence reinforces identity.

The identity produces similar behavior.

The cycle continues.

Transformation occurs when the cycle is interrupted consciously.

---

# My Experience As A Coach

After decades of coaching individuals, entrepreneurs, athletes, performers, executives, and teams, I have observed a consistent truth.

The greatest breakthroughs rarely occur when strategy changes.

The greatest breakthroughs occur when identity changes.

The moment someone genuinely sees themselves differently, behavior begins changing naturally.

What once felt difficult becomes normal.

What once felt impossible becomes achievable.

What once felt uncomfortable becomes familiar.

The self-image shifts.

Reality follows.

---

# The Olympic Lesson

While working with elite athletes, I repeatedly witnessed a powerful distinction.

The athletes who performed at the highest levels did not merely practice different actions.

They possessed different identities.

They expected success.

They expected excellence.

They expected performance.

Those expectations influenced behavior.

Behavior influenced results.

Results reinforced identity.

The cycle accelerated upward.

Champions often win twice.

First internally.

Then externally.

# The Relationship Between Meaning And Self-Image

Meaning creates identity.

Identity creates self-image.

Consider two interpretations of failure.

### Meaning One

"I failed because I am not capable."

### Meaning Two

"I failed because I am learning."

The event is identical.

The identity formed is different.

The resulting self-image is different.

The future becomes different.

This is why meaning matters.

The meanings we repeat eventually become who we believe we are.

---

# The Relationship Between Thought And Self-Image

Self-image does not appear suddenly.

It develops through repeated thought.

```text
Thought
    ↓
Belief
    ↓
Identity
    ↓
Self-Image
    ↓
Reality
```

Thought creates frequency.

Frequency influences perception.

Perception influences meaning.

Meaning influences identity.

Identity stabilizes as self-image.

Reality then reflects the self-image.

# Self-Image And Love

One of the deepest wounds many people carry is the belief that they are not enough.

Not smart enough.

Not worthy enough.

Not successful enough.

Not lovable enough.

Much of human suffering emerges from this distorted self-image.

Love dissolves these distortions.

When individuals begin viewing themselves through the lens of love, identity changes.

When identity changes, life changes.

This is one reason the question:

> How Would Love Respond?

is so transformational.

It changes how we see ourselves.

---

# Rewriting The Self-Image

Transformation requires more than positive thinking.

It requires identity reconstruction.

The process includes:

1. Becoming aware of the existing self-image.
2. Identifying limiting beliefs.
3. Challenging inherited conclusions.
4. Creating empowering meanings.
5. Building evidence through action.
6. Reinforcing a new identity repeatedly.
7. Living consistently from the new identity.

Lasting transformation is identity-based.

---

# Practical Application

Ask yourself:

- Who do I currently believe I am?
- What evidence supports that belief?
- What evidence contradicts it?
- What identity would support my highest vision?
- What would that version of me think?
- How would that version of me act?
- What would love believe about me?

Then begin acting from that identity today.

Not after the results arrive.

Before they arrive.
